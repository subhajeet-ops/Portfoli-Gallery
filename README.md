# ✦ The Gallery

> *"Every artwork deserves more than a scroll. It deserves a room."*

**The Gallery** is an interactive museum experience built entirely with **HTML, CSS, and Vanilla JavaScript**. Instead of showcasing artwork through a conventional portfolio website, it invites visitors to step inside a handcrafted virtual gallery where every room tells its own story.

Walk through grand museum halls, accompanied by a small companion dog, wander between exhibitions, and experience each artwork as though it were hanging in a real gallery.

**🌐 Live Demo**
https://subhagallery.vercel.app/

---

# The Idea

Most portfolio websites ask visitors to scroll.

I wanted mine to be explored.

The Gallery began with a simple thought: *What if a portfolio felt like visiting an art museum instead of browsing a webpage?*

Every interaction was designed around that idea. You don't jump between pages—you walk through hallways. You don't browse thumbnails—you approach framed artwork. Every room has its own architecture, atmosphere, lighting, and personality, making each collection feel like a separate exhibition rather than another section of a website.

---

# The Journey

The experience begins inside a quiet museum hallway.

Three grand doors stand before you.

The first opens into a warm classical gallery filled with traditional paintings, illuminated by soft golden light and surrounded by rich wooden interiors that echo the feeling of an old museum.

As you continue your walk, another doorway leads somewhere completely different.

The familiar warmth slowly disappears. The walls darken into shades of midnight blue, stars begin to shimmer overhead, and golden astronomical patterns emerge across the architecture. What was once a museum now feels like an observatory suspended beneath the night sky.

This second gallery houses my collection of digital illustrations. Every frame glows softly against the celestial surroundings, encouraging visitors to slow down, explore, and uncover the story behind each artwork.

The transition between these two spaces isn't just visual—it represents the contrast between traditional and digital art, each presented within an environment created specifically for that collection.

# Features

* Immersive museum-inspired experience
* Interactive hallway navigation
* Two fully explorable exhibition rooms
* Walking character accompanied by a companion dog
* Responsive controls for desktop and mobile
* Interactive artwork viewer with descriptions and quotes
* Smooth room transitions
* Atmospheric lighting and visual effects
* Custom-built animations using pure CSS and JavaScript
* No frameworks or external libraries

---

# Controls

| Action              | Control                     |
| ------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Walk inside hallway | Click anywhere on the floor |
| Enter a gallery     | Click a door                |
| Move inside a room  | Arrow Keys or **A / D**     |
| Inspect an artwork  | Click the frame             |
| Close artwork       | **Esc** or **×**            |
| Leave a room        | Walk toward the exit        |
| Mobile              | On-screen movement controls |

# Inspiration

The Gallery draws inspiration from the quiet atmosphere of museums and the idea that digital experiences can feel physical.

Rather than presenting artwork through grids and galleries, this project attempts to recreate the feeling of wandering through an exhibition—pausing in front of a painting, taking a few steps forward, and discovering what lies in the next room.
